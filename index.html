Introduction

  Formula One (F1) cars are among the fastest vehicles on the planet, going as fast as 
  220mph (DIRECTV Insider, 2025), the exhilaration of watching these cars race against each 
  other has garnered a vast group of dedicated fans all over the world. An essential factor that 
  allows these vehicles to travel as fast as possible is aerodynamics (Pandit and Day, 2021), 
  which is the study of forces acting on objects moving through the air (Hall, 2023). But how 
  exactly does making an F1 car more aerodynamic increase the speed at which it can travel? 
  There are many areas in an F1 car that are greatly improved purely by the principles of 
  aerodynamics, such areas include downforce, DRS, cooling, stability among others. These 
  all, in one way or another, contribute to increasing the speed of the vehicle for the greatest 
  chance of winning a race. This article will go into more depth on these concepts and explain 
  how aerodynamic principles help F1 cars achieve high speeds.
